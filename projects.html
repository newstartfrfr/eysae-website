Overview

Project rationale and purpose

EYSAE addresses youth unemployment, limited entrepreneurial support and environmental pressure by linking youth work, sustainable entrepreneurship, digital learning and international cooperation.

Why the project is relevant

The proposal is rooted in a needs analysis focused on employment barriers, weak support structures, low civic participation and limited awareness of sustainable production and consumption.

What the project is trying to change

It aims to strengthen youth organisations, build practical entrepreneurial skills, improve civic participation and create durable digital resources and visibility outputs.

What stays after the activities

The long-term ambition is continued access to training resources, stronger cooperation links, mentorship pathways and wider use of sustainable entrepreneurship methods.

Implementation flow

Implementation logic

The proposal describes a step-by-step methodology that can be shown as a clean implementation path for visitors.

Phase 1

Foundation and strategic planning

Kick-off coordination, work distribution, quality assurance setup and thematic alignment across the consortium.

Phase 2

Knowledge exchange and local action

Study visits, training sessions and local follow-up workshops transfer learning into practice inside each partner country.

Phase 3

Mobility and international exchange

International activities connect youth workers and participants through thematic exchange, best-practice sharing and applied learning.

Phase 4

Digitalisation, visibility and sustainability

The project extends its reach through digital resources, media content, public communication and long-term access to results.
