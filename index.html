Context

Context the Project

EYSAE is a capacity-building project focused on the Southern Mediterranean region. It helps young people and youth organisations develop entrepreneurial, digital and sustainability-related competences with a strong link to agriculture and green economic opportunities.

Why this project matters

The project responds to high youth unemployment, limited access to quality training, underused agricultural potential and weak support structures for sustainable entrepreneurship, especially in rural and marginalised contexts.

What EYSAE delivers

EYSAE combines training, study visits, youth exchange, local follow-up activities, digital resources and public dissemination so learning can move from international cooperation into local practice.

Objectives

Project Objectives

01

Strengthen the capacity of youth organisations

Equip youth organisations with methods, tools and cooperation models so they can better support young entrepreneurs and non-formal learning.

02

Build entrepreneurial and digital skills

Give young people practical skills in entrepreneurship, financial literacy, digital tools and sustainable business development.

03

Increase mobility, exchange and civic participation

Use transnational learning, study visits, exchange formats and civic engagement activities to connect youth, youth workers and stakeholders across countries.

04

Create lasting digital and public-facing results

Develop an online platform, media materials and dissemination outputs that keep knowledge accessible beyond the project lifecycle.
